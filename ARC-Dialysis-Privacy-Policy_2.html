1. Who We Are

ARC Dialysis ("ARC Dialysis," "we," "us," or "our") is a privately owned, minority-owned kidney care practice incorporated in the State of Florida. We operate 12 outpatient dialysis centers across Miami-Dade, Broward, Polk, Lee, and Hendry counties. Our corporate office is located at 4960 SW 72nd Ave #208, Miami, FL 33155.

ARC Dialysis is a Covered Entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations (the HIPAA Privacy Rule, 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164). This means we are required by law to maintain the privacy of Protected Health Information (PHI) and to provide patients with notice of our legal duties and privacy practices with respect to PHI.

This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through our website at https://arcdialysis.com and through other digital communications with us. For information about how we handle PHI in the context of medical treatment, please refer to our Notice of Privacy Practices available at your ARC Dialysis center.

2. Information We Collect

We collect different types of information depending on how you interact with our website:

Information You Provide to Us

When you contact us, submit a form, or communicate with our care teams, you may provide:

  • Contact information — such as your name, email address, phone number, and mailing address.
  • Patient intake information — when you complete our online patient forms, such as demographic information, insurance details, and referring physician information.
  • Employment application information — if you apply for a position through our website, you may submit a resume, cover letter, and professional history.
  • General inquiries — questions or comments you submit through our contact form or by email.

Please note: We ask that you do not submit sensitive health information (such as diagnoses, lab values, or detailed medical history) through general website contact forms. For clinical matters, please call your ARC Dialysis center directly.

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we and our service providers may automatically collect certain information about your device and your visit, including:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location (city/region level).
  • Browser type, version, and operating system.
  • Pages you visit, links you click, and time spent on pages.
  • Referring URLs (the page from which you arrived at our website).
  • Date and time of your visit.
  • Device identifiers and mobile device information.

This information is collected through cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies. See Section 6 (Cookies and Tracking Technologies) for more details.

Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, such as referring physicians, hospital systems, or other healthcare providers, when they refer you to our services. This clinical referral information is handled in accordance with our Notice of Privacy Practices and applicable HIPAA regulations.

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit it. They help the website remember your preferences and allow us to understand how visitors use our site. Similar technologies include web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage.

How We Use Cookies

ARC Dialysis uses the following categories of cookies and tracking tools:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies — Essential to the operation of our website. Without these, the website cannot function properly. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics and Performance Cookies — We use Google Analytics (currently UA-213823725-1) to collect aggregated, anonymized data about how visitors use our website — such as which pages are visited most frequently and how users navigate the site. This helps us improve the website experience. Google Analytics may set cookies on your device. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout (opens in new window).
  • Functional Cookies — Remember your preferences, such as language or region, to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Third-Party Cookies — Embedded content from third-party providers (such as Google Maps or YouTube) may place their own cookies on your device. These are governed by those providers' privacy policies.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. You may be able to set your browser to refuse certain cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more information on managing cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org (opens in new window).

8. Data Security

ARC Dialysis takes reasonable administrative, technical, and physical measures to protect the information we collect from loss, theft, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. These measures include:

  • Secure Sockets Layer (SSL/TLS) encryption for data transmitted through our website.
  • Access controls limiting staff access to personal information to those with a legitimate need.
  • Regular review of data collection, storage, and processing practices.
  • Physical safeguards at our facilities and those of our service providers.

However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we work hard to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you believe your information has been compromised, please contact us immediately at (305) 448-6261 or info@arcdialysis.com.

In the event of a data breach involving unsecured PHI, ARC Dialysis will comply with applicable HIPAA breach notification requirements, including notifying affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by law.
