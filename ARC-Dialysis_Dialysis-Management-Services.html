What many dialysis leaders are really dealing with

Hospitals, private nephrology groups, and dialysis program leaders rarely need more reminders about the mission. They need a practical way to stop operational complexity from draining time, straining teams, and slowing performance.

Common pressure points include:

  • Revenue cycle leakage from missed charges, claim delays, denials, and collections friction.
  • Staffing instability caused by recruiting delays, onboarding bottlenecks, and retention pressure.
  • Compliance burden tied to CMS Conditions for Coverage, HIPAA, state licensure, audits, and QAPI oversight.
  • Operational blind spots when reporting, technology, and workflow visibility are fragmented.
  • Leadership distraction when physicians and administrators spend too much time on back-office demands instead of patient care and strategy.

ARC Dialysis can manage the full dialysis service line or deliver targeted dialysis practice support where the strain is greatest. Your organization keeps ownership, physician leadership, and strategic direction while ARC helps carry the operational burden.

  • More structure around dialysis operations, staffing, reporting, compliance, and financial performance.

Modular Management & Back-Office Services

ARC Dialysis can run the full operational platform or provide targeted dialysis practice services where your team needs the most help. Each area below supports stronger dialysis operations, clearer accountability, and more time for patient care.

Revenue Cycle & Collections

Charge capture, dialysis claims management, denials follow-up, and collections support across Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial plans with a compliance-oriented approach.

Accounting & Financial Controls

AP, AR, payroll, budgeting, and dialysis-specific financial reporting that improves visibility, control, and decision-making.

Operations & Logistics

Day-to-day dialysis operations, supply chain planning, staffing optimization, workflow efficiency, and throughput improvement built for real service line demands.

Technology & Data

IT infrastructure, EHR support, and analytics that help clinical and administrative teams work from the same real-time information.

Payor Strategy

Support for Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care, and commercial contracting aligned with your service footprint, referral flow, and growth goals.

Clinical Education

Role-based education for dialysis nurses and patient care technicians focused on safety, infection prevention, communication, and operational consistency.

Quality & Accreditation

Performance monitoring, adverse event review, QAPI support, and continuous improvement that strengthens dialysis regulatory readiness and accreditation efforts.

Compliance & Risk

Policies, audits, team training, and risk management support covering CMS Conditions for Coverage, HIPAA, state licensure, and ongoing dialysis oversight.

HR & Recruiting

Credentialing, hiring, onboarding, and retention planning tailored for dialysis nurses, technicians, and operational support teams.

Acute Hospital Bedside Care

Outpatient Dialysis Centers

Operational and clinical support for outpatient dialysis programs including staffing infrastructure, quality monitoring, compliance support, workflow oversight, and stabilization.

Home Dialysis Programs

Quality, Safety & Standards Framework
