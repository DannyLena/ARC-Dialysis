If you or a loved one are approaching the need for dialysis, selecting the right dialysis center is an important healthcare decision. At Apollo Renal Center, our team is committed to providing compassionate, personalized kidney care in a supportive and comfortable environment.

Kidney Care in the Heart of Miami's Medical Campus

Apollo Renal Center brings compassionate, high-quality dialysis care to Miami's world-renowned Health District—steps from Jackson Memorial Hospital, the University of Miami medical campus, and the Civic Center Metrorail station. As part of ARC Dialysis, Florida's largest kidney care network with 12 centers statewide, we deliver the clinical excellence you'd expect from a location in the second-largest health district in the nation, combined with the personal attention of a privately owned, minority-owned company that treats every patient like family.

Whether you live in Little Havana, work downtown, or travel from Overtown or Allapattah for your treatments, our bilingual care team is here to support you. We understand that living with chronic kidney disease means navigating complex medical needs—and we've positioned ourselves at the center of Miami's healthcare ecosystem so you can coordinate your kidney care with specialists, hospital visits, and follow-up appointments all in one convenient area.

Why Health District Patients Choose Apollo Renal Center

  • Steps from Jackson Memorial Hospital — Seamless coordination between hospital care and outpatient dialysis, with easy transitions for patients recently discharged or receiving ongoing specialty care at Jackson, UM Hospital, or the VA Medical Center.
  • Civic Center Metrorail accessible — Take the Green or Orange Line directly to Civic Center Station. We're a short walk from the platform, making treatment accessible whether you're coming from Kendall, Hialeah, or anywhere along the Metrorail corridor.
  • Fully bilingual care team — Receive your care in English or Spanish. Our staff reflects the diverse communities we serve, from Little Havana's Cuban heritage to the multicultural fabric of Overtown and Allapattah.
  • Part of Florida's largest kidney care network — With 12 ARC Dialysis centers across the state, your records travel with you. Visiting family in Bonita Springs? Need coverage while in Winter Haven? We've got you covered.
  • Flexible scheduling for working patients — Early morning and evening shifts available so you can maintain your work schedule, whether you're commuting downtown or working in the Health District itself.
  • — Our center meets the highest national standards for patient safety and quality care.

Getting to Apollo Renal Center

By Metrorail (Green or Orange Line)

The Civic Center Metrorail Station on NW 12th Avenue is a short walk from our center. Both the Green Line (to Palmetto/Miami Airport) and Orange Line (to MIC/Miami Airport) stop here. The station connects you to the entire Metrorail system, from Dadeland in the south to Palmetto and the Tri-Rail transfer station in the north. For patients coming from Hialeah, you can catch the Green Line at any station and ride directly to Civic Center.

By Metrobus

Multiple Metrobus routes serve the Health District area, including Route 12 (along NW 12th Avenue from Mercy Hospital to Northside), Route 20, and Route 32. The Health District Trolley provides free circulator service connecting Jackson Memorial Hospital, UM Hospital, Sylvester Cancer Center, and the Civic Center Metrorail station—making it easy to combine your dialysis appointment with other medical visits.

By Car

From I-95, take Exit 3A for the Dolphin Expressway (836 West) toward Miami International Airport, then exit onto NW 14th Street and head toward NW 3rd Street. From the west via the Dolphin Expressway, take the NW 17th Avenue exit toward the Civic Center hospitals. Street parking and nearby garage options are available throughout the Health District.

Paratransit & Ride Services

Miami-Dade Special Transportation Service (STS) provides door-to-door paratransit for eligible patients throughout the county. Many of our patients also use Uber or Lyft, with the Health District being one of Miami's most accessible drop-off locations. Need help arranging transportation? Our patient care coordinators can connect you with resources.

Comprehensive Kidney Care Services

At Apollo Renal Center, we provide the full spectrum of dialysis services to support patients at every stage of their kidney care journey:

In-Center Hemodialysis

Our comfortable treatment chairs, modern dialysis equipment, and attentive nursing staff make your three-to-four-hour sessions as relaxed as possible. Free WiFi and personal TVs help pass the time.

Home Dialysis Training & Support

For patients interested in peritoneal dialysis or home hemodialysis, we offer comprehensive training and ongoing clinical support so you can treat at home with confidence.

Hospital Coordination

Our proximity to Jackson Memorial, UM Hospital, the VA Medical Center, and Holtz Children's Hospital means we work closely with your hospital care team for seamless transitions between inpatient and outpatient dialysis.

Vascular Access Management

We coordinate with vascular surgeons in the Health District to ensure your fistula or graft is functioning optimally, with regular monitoring and timely intervention when needed.

Nutritional Counseling

Our team helps you understand how diet affects your kidney health and dialysis outcomes, with guidance tailored to the foods and flavors of Miami's diverse culinary traditions.

Social Services Support

Living with kidney disease affects every part of your life. Our social workers connect you with transportation assistance, insurance navigation, and community resources throughout Miami-Dade County.

Why Your Dialysis Center Matters

Your kidneys work around the clock to filter waste, balance fluids, and regulate blood pressure. When chronic kidney disease progresses to end-stage renal disease (ESRD), hemodialysis takes over these essential functions—typically three sessions per week, three to four hours each session. That's a significant commitment, which is why choosing the right dialysis center matters so much.

At Apollo Renal Center, we believe dialysis should fit into your life, not the other way around. Our Health District location means you can combine treatment with appointments at Jackson, UM specialists, or other medical providers in the area. Our bilingual staff ensures nothing gets lost in translation. And our flexible scheduling helps working patients maintain their careers and independence.

Who We Serve & What to Expect

We provide dialysis care for patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) stages 4-5, end-stage renal disease (ESRD), acute kidney injury (AKI), and those awaiting or recovering from kidney transplant. Whether you're newly diagnosed or transferring from another center, here's what your care journey looks like with us:

  1. Initial Consultation — We'll review your medical history, discuss your treatment options, and answer every question you and your family have about dialysis.
  2. Care Planning — Your nephrologist and our clinical team design a personalized treatment plan based on your kidney function, lifestyle, and health goals.
  3. Access Preparation — If you need vascular access created, we coordinate with Health District vascular surgeons to ensure your fistula, graft, or catheter is ready when you are.
  4. First Treatment — Our nurses guide you through your first dialysis session step by step, monitoring your comfort and response throughout.
  5. Ongoing Care — Regular treatment sessions, monthly lab work, and continuous support from our care team to optimize your health and quality of life.
  6. Life Integration — We work with you to schedule treatments around work, family, and the things that matter most to you.
